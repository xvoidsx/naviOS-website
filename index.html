> the_top_panel (waybar)

wired launcher (Δ): Click for the Nightshade Menu.

workspaces: Click numbers to jump. Hover to see window counts.

time and date:
• Click Day (Alt+C shortcut) for calcurse.
• Click Time for tty-clock.
• Click Date (Super+C shortcut) for gsimplecal.

volume: Scroll over icon to adjust. Left-click to mute.

idle inhibitor (👁): Toggle to prevent screen timeout during long reads or compiles.

    Welcome to the Wired ~

    navi is a minimal distribution built upon Debian Testing, featuring our wired tiling window management setup. Inspired by the Serial Experiments Lain aesthetic, it is designed to be a personal portal into the decentralized Web.

    naviOS is a love letter to the retro age of computing, to free and open-source software, and to the uniqueness of Linux. It embraces a minimal, tiling-first and terminal-centric way of computing, with your operating system flying underneath your fingers as you navigate using your keybindings.

    The operating system teems with life, as if it were receiving signals from the Wired itself. The panel breathes as if it were an RGB keyboard, cycling through the colors of the nightshadeNeon theme. Beautiful, lo-fi, animated wallpapers glow behind your terminals. naviOS is not only functional, but also beautiful, with an aesthetic quite unmatched by any other operating system.

    Whether you're programming on the smallweb, gaming, streaming, producing music, creating art, writing, or anything else, naviOS is designed to get out of your way and provide you a gorgeous, intuitive space in which to work and play. Once you've gotten used to the navi way of doing things, you'll find it hard to use other environments again!

    Welcome to the Wired, friend. We're glad you're here.
